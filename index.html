About PODER

The PODER program aims to prepare students with academic potential for success in a college preparatory path. The goal of PODER is to support students in being successful in challenging courses that lead to enrollment success in college. The program provides a support for first-generation college bound students in connecting identity with academic purpose and a continued commitment to education.

Program Activities & Objectives

PODER provides:

- Summer Bridge Programs and Classes

- Afterschool and In Class Tutoring

- College Talks and Field Trips

- SAT/ACT Preparation and SAT School Day

- Mentoring of incoming students

Poder Objectives:

- Provide students with instruction and skills important to academic success

- Provide support for success in college preparatory coursework

- Prepare students to be leaders on campus and participate in campus clubs and activities

- Motivate students to seek a college education

Apply to PODER

Applicants must meet the following requirements to be considered for the program

1. Will be the first to attend college.

2. Have a minimum 2.0 grade average.

3. Have the desire to work hard in challenging courses and attend a four-year college or university.

4. Are willing to enroll in rigorous college-prepatory sequence of courses, including Honors and Advanced Placement courses

Applications must be turned in February of 2025

Students will receive notice for interviews the following month, and the status of application will be revealed in April.
